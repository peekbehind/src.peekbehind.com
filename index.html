About

PeekBehind shows the mouse cursors of your latest users as ghosts layered on top of each page of your web site.

This visualization, inspired from video games, is a modern and efficient way to gain insights in the patterns of use of interactive applications.

As stated by usability expert Jakob Nielsen, as little as 5 users are sufficient to identify the strength and shortcomings of a user interface.

With PeekBehind, as soon as you navigate to a page of your own web site, you see a replay of the activity recorded for the 5 most recent visitors of the page, shown simultaneously.

PeekBehind respects the privacy of your visitors and records nothing when the "Do Not Track" preference of the browser is checked. You can take advantage of this exception to avoid recording the activity of yourself and your own employees.

You can build Open Source PeekBehind yourself for free and contribute with code and feedback.

You can install PeekBehind for WordPress directly from your WordPress administration area. You will view replays of user activity in any page of your site when you are logged in as a WordPress administrator.

You can add Common PeekBehind to a page of your site by pasting a snippet of JavaScript. You will view replays of user activity in these pages when you are logged in on Common PeekBehind.

You can hire me to help your team integrate a Custom PeekBehind with a large Web site or a Web application.

Because Open Source PeekBehind is released under MIT license for the code and Creative Commons Attribution for the graphics and documentation, there are no strings attached, even for commercial use.

Because you are hosting PeekBehind for WordPress on the same server as your site, you get optimum performance and no recurring cost.

Because Common PeekBehind is a shared service, it is not adapted to sites with high traffic or thousands of pages to monitor, which should consider a Custom PeekBehind instead.

Because a Custom PeekBehind is adapted to your specific requirements it offers higher fidelity for recordings of user activity.
